Edward Abbey: A Life

"May your trails be crooked, winding, lonesome, dangerous, leading to the most amazing view. May your mountains rise into and above the clouds."
- Desert Solitaire, by Edward Abbey

Overview

Edward Abbey was born in Pennsylvania on January 29th, 1927, and died on March 14th, 1989.

Best known for his conservation work and writing, Edward Abbey has long been considered a towering figure among environmentalists, known for his uncompromising conservation stances and anarchistic tendencies. His writing, so eloquent and powerful, evoked adventure in his readers, and his crass humor and middle finger to the establishment provided a sense of camaraderie.

His spirit lives on through his writings, and his soul in the desert; disregarding all state and federal laws, fittingly enough, his friends buried him illegally in the Cabeza Prieta Desert.

His last message, to give to anyone asking about his final words: "No comment." 1

Notable Works:

  • The Brave Cowboy (1958)
  • Desert Solitaire (1968)
  • Slickrock (1971)
  • The Monkey Wrench Gang (1975)
  • Abbey’s Road (1979)
  • The Fool’s Progress (1988)
  • Hayduke Lives! (1990)

His best known work is perhaps Desert Solitaire, written in 1968 about his experiences in Utah and lauded for its fierce, poetic imagery. Another is The Monkey Wrench Gang, a book about environmentalists who sought to protect the wilderness using guerilla-like tactics. Both are now considered necessary reads for any adventurer seeking to understand and protect this beautiful planet we call home before it's too late.
